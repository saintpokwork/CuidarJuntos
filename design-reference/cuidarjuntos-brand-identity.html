Strategic Gap — The White Space CuidarJuntos Can Own

Every competitor occupies one of two poles: cold health-tech (institutional blue, clinical aesthetics) or dated care-service (house icons, literal warmth). No one in the Portuguese or Iberian market offers a modern, premium-light, warmly organised visual identity rooted in family connection. CuidarJuntos can own the intersection of contemporary Portuguese craft and calm digital care — the visual language of a well-designed home, not a hospital or a brochure.

Phase 2 — Brand Foundations

Strategy & Positioning

The strategic scaffold that every visual decision is built upon.

Brand Personality
Sereno Organizado Próximo Moderno Fiável

Calm without being cold. Organised without being clinical. Close to the family without being sentimental. Modern without feeling foreign. Reliable without feeling corporate.

Brand Archetype

O Cuidador
The Caregiver

CuidarJuntos is fundamentally a Caregiver archetype — it protects, organises, and nurtures. Unlike the Hero (which conquers) or the Sage (which teaches), the Caregiver quietly enables others to show up better for the people they love. This archetype justifies the calm palette, the warm typography, and the gentle symbol — it should never shout.

"CuidarJuntos é a ferramenta de organização familiar que ajuda famílias portuguesas a cuidar dos seus entes queridos com mais calma, clareza e união."

Emotional Tone

Families using CuidarJuntos should feel relieved — like they've handed a difficult coordination burden to a calm, capable system. Not anxious, not overwhelmed. Quietly in control.

Value Perception

Premium-light — modern and considered, but not exclusive or intimidating. Accessible to a 55-year-old adult child managing care for an 80-year-old parent. Feels trustworthy, not expensive.

Cultural Anchor

Rooted in Portuguese visual sensibility — earthy warmth, calm restraint, the Azulejo tradition of beautiful organisation. Not generically "European health-tech."

Phase 3 — Creative Direction

The Design Decision

One opinionated creative direction chosen to stand apart from every competitor identified in the audit.

Typography Direction

DM Sans — a humanist geometric sans-serif with warm, slightly optical corrections. It reads at small sizes (app labels, dashboard numbers) while carrying enough personality to feel like a brand choice, not a default. Paired with DM Serif Display (italic) for heading moments that need warmth. The combination is modern without clinical sterility, and warm without being childish. Both are free, Portuguese-legible, and web-optimised.

Colour Direction

Forest green as primary — not clinical, not corporate, not cold. The green of the Portuguese Serra, of aloe vera, of a kitchen garden. It reads as calm, organic, and trustworthy. Offset with a warm Azulejo terracota accent — the warmth of Portuguese tiles. Background in areia (warm off-white), not sterile white. Together, this palette is completely unoccupied in the care-tech market.

Symbol Direction

The icon is a care circle monogram — a stylised "CJ" where the C opens gently around a soft inner arc representing continuity, togetherness, and the quiet enclosure of family care. It reads as: a circle (wholeness, family), a C (brand initial), and an arc inside (J initial, inner protection). At small sizes it reads as a calm, geometric app icon. It deliberately avoids hands, hearts, crosses, houses, and stick figures.

Differentiation

No competitor uses forest green + terracota + warm areia together. No Portuguese care brand has a monogram-led icon instead of literal imagery. No international app speaks with this cultural specificity. The identity will be immediately recognisable as belonging to the Portuguese family context while being modern enough to sit in an app store next to European health-tech products.

Phase 6 — Design System

Logo Usage Guidelines

Rules to protect the identity across all applications.

Clear Space
← X →

Minimum clear space = the height of the "C" arc on all sides. Never crowd the logo with other elements. On small formats (sidebar, favicon), use the icon-only mark.

Minimum Size
120px — min print
80px — too small

Minimum wordmark: 120px wide (print: 30mm). Below this, use icon-only mark. Digital minimum for icon: 20px × 20px.

✓ SIM — Faça isto
  • Use the wordmark on white, warm off-white, or forest green backgrounds
  • Use the white version on the brand gradient and dark backgrounds
  • Use the icon-only mark for app icons, favicons, and sidebar collapsed states
  • Maintain the two-line wordmark split: "Cuidar" bold, "Juntos" light
  • Use the terracota dot as it appears — never recolour it to green
  • Allow the Portuguese script conventions to inform spacing
✗ NÃO — Evite isto
  • Do not place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds
  • Do not stretch, squash, or rotate the logo
  • Do not add drop shadows or decorative effects to the logo
  • Do not use the wordmark smaller than 120px wide
  • Do not recolour the icon to terracota or change the arc colours
  • Do not place the dark wordmark on the forest green background — use white version
  • Do not recreate the logo in text — always use the SVG

Tom de Voz

Cinco regras para o copy português — para que a marca soe tão bem quanto parece.

1

Fale com calma e clareza

Use português direto e contemporâneo. Sem jargão médico, sem linguagem técnica desnecessária. "Adicione os medicamentos do seu pai" — não "Registe a terapêutica do utente". A família é o utilizador, não o profissional.

2

Não use medo como motivação

Evite formulações baseadas em ansiedade ou urgência negativa. Não: "Não perca os medicamentos do seu pai." Sim: "Tenha sempre em dia os medicamentos de quem ama." O tom é calmo e encorajador — nunca alarmista.

3

Fale para a família, não apenas para o cuidador

O utilizador principal pode ser a filha, o filho, o neto ou o cônjuge. Use "a sua família", "todos juntos", "partilhem". A aplicação é uma ferramenta de coordenação colectiva — não um diário privado.

4

Seja concreto — números e acções, não adjectivos

Não: "Uma solução completa e intuitiva para gestão de cuidados." Sim: "3 medicamentos hoje. 1 consulta amanhã. Toda a família informada." O copy deve refletir a utilidade real do produto em linguagem familiar.

5

Nunca pareça um hospital

Evite: "registo clínico", "historial médico", "plataforma de saúde", "prescrição". Prefira: "notas de cuidados", "informação da família", "fichas do seu familiar", "agenda de consultas". A linguagem deve ser doméstica e calorosa — não clínica.
